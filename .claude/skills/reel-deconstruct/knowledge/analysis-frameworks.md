# Analysis frameworks — binding taxonomies for reel teardowns

Use this vocabulary verbatim so every teardown is consistent and comparable. When a reel doesn't fit cleanly, name the closest archetype and call it a hybrid — don't invent a brand-new label every time.

---

## A. The beat sheet (skeleton)

A talking-head reel is a sequence of *functional beats*, not paragraphs. Map the transcript onto these. Most reels are 5–9 beats; short ones collapse several into one line. Note which beats are **skipped** — omission is a deliberate choice.

| Beat | Typical timing | Function |
|------|----------------|----------|
| **Hook** | 0–3s | Stop the scroll. Earn the next 2 seconds. |
| **Re-hook / Context** | 3–7s | Confirm "this is for you," raise the stakes, or restate sharper. Stops the second-scroll. |
| **Tension / Problem** | — | The itch. The gap between where the viewer is and wants to be. |
| **Turn / Reframe** | — | The pivot — "but actually," the contrarian flip, the reveal. The single most important beat. |
| **Mechanism / Proof** | — | *Why* the turn is true: a story, a number, a logic chain, a credential. |
| **Escalation / Examples** | — | Stacking — list items, more proof, raising the emotional temperature. |
| **Payoff / Insight** | — | The takeaway the viewer leaves with — the line they'd screenshot. |
| **CTA / Landing** | last 2–5s | Explicit ("comment X", "follow for Y") or implicit (a loop-closing line that earns the save). |

For each beat record: **timestamp range** (if known), **the actual line**, **its function**.

## B. Framework archetypes (name the structure)

Name the reel's repeatable structure with one of these. State the "formula" in one line.

1. **False-Belief Demolition** — "You think X. You're wrong. Here's Y." Names a belief the viewer holds, detonates it, replaces it.
2. **Contrarian Confession** — "Unpopular opinion…" / "I used to believe X until…" Status earned by saying the thing others won't.
3. **Hidden Mechanism** — "Here's what's *actually* happening when…" Reveals the gears behind something familiar. Feels like insider access.
4. **Status-Quo Autopsy** — "Why everyone doing X is failing." Dissects a common approach and pronounces it dead.
5. **Permission Slip** — "You're allowed to…" / "You don't have to…" Relieves guilt or pressure. High save/share among the anxious.
6. **Identity Flip** — "Stop being the person who X. Become the person who Y." Sells a new self-image, not a tactic.
7. **Origin-Story Lesson** — "I did X, here's what it taught me." Borrowed credibility through narrative; the lesson rides the story.
8. **Listicle Escalation** — "3 things / 5 signs / 7 mistakes…" Each item a mini open-loop; structure does the retention.
9. **Callout** — "If you're someone who…" Names a specific person so precisely they feel seen and have to engage.
10. **Future-Pace** — "Imagine if in 90 days you…" Paints the after-state vividly so the viewer wants the bridge.
11. **The Receipt** — leads with proof/result ("I made $X / grew to Y"), then reverse-engineers how. Result buys attention.
12. **Myth-vs-Reality** — pairs the lie and the truth back to back, often rhythmically. Built for rewatch + share.

Hybrids are common (e.g. "Contrarian Confession → Hidden Mechanism"). Name both.

## C. Hook taxonomy (why the first 3s work)

Classify the opening mechanism. A hook usually stacks 2–3 of these.

- **Curiosity gap** — opens a loop the brain needs closed ("The reason your reels flop has nothing to do with the algorithm").
- **Pattern interrupt** — unexpected visual/verbal/tonal jolt that breaks the scroll rhythm.
- **Stakes / cost** — names what's being lost right now ("You're leaving money on the table every day you…").
- **Specificity / number** — a precise figure or timeframe that signals "real, not fluff" ("I went from 200 to 40k in 90 days").
- **Identity callout** — names the exact viewer ("If you're a founder who's great offline but invisible online…").
- **Contrarian claim** — states the opposite of accepted wisdom; the friction holds attention.
- **Negative framing** — "Stop doing X" / "The mistake that's killing your…" — loss aversion outpulls gain.
- **Authority drop** — credential or result in the first line that earns the right to be heard.

For the teardown: name the mechanism(s) and say in 1–2 sentences *why it stops the thumb* for this specific audience.

## D. Audience psychology lenses (what the reel DOES for the viewer)

This is the core of the teardown. Run the reel through every lens:

1. **Target viewer** — who *specifically* (not "entrepreneurs" — "the freelancer who's busy but stuck at $5k months"). The narrower the implied target, the stronger the reel usually is.
2. **The itch (belief/tension engaged)** — what unresolved tension or held belief does it poke? People watch to relieve tension, not to learn.
3. **Emotional before → after** — the state shift the viewer gets in 30–60s. (e.g. anxious-and-confused → clear-and-permitted.) Name both states.
4. **Identity work** — what self-image does it *flatter* ("smart people already know this"), or what *threat* does it neutralize ("it's not your fault, it's the system")?
5. **Objection pre-empted** — the "yeah but…" it silences before the viewer can raise it.
6. **Social currency (why save/share)** — does it (a) make the sharer look smart, (b) give them language for something they felt but couldn't say, (c) validate their existing side, or (d) serve as a save-for-later tool? Shares come from *identity expression*, saves from *utility*.
7. **The promise vs the payoff** — what it implicitly promised in the hook vs what it actually delivered. Gaps here are where trust is won or lost.

## E. Retention & delivery mechanics

How the reel keeps people to the end:

- **Open-loop economy** — loops opened early and paid off late; count them and note where each closes.
- **Pacing & sentence rhythm** — short punchy lines vs long; where it speeds up/slows down; the "but / so / because" engine driving momentum.
- **Pattern interrupts** — cuts, tone shifts, B-roll, on-screen text, props — anything resetting attention every few seconds.
- **Specificity density** — concrete numbers, names, examples vs vague claims. Specifics buy belief.
- **Callbacks** — does the ending echo the hook to close the loop and earn the rewatch/save?
- **List/escalation scaffolding** — numbered structure that promises a known endpoint, reducing scroll-away.
- **The drop-off risk** — the single weakest moment where attention most likely breaks (a slow middle, an over-long setup, a buried turn). Always name one.

---

## F. Transfer template (abstract, don't recreate)

End by abstracting the reel into a reusable fill-in-the-blank skeleton, e.g.:

> **[Identity callout]** + **[contrarian claim about their #1 belief]** → **[name the real mechanism]** → **[one proof/story]** → **[reframe as identity]** → **[implicit CTA / loop close]**

Then a short **Transfer note**: would this framework work for Shekhar's audience (offline-strong, online-invisible founders)? What single variable changes (the belief poked, the proof type, the identity sold)? Keep it structural — placeholders, not a finished script. Recreation is explicitly out of scope for this skill.
